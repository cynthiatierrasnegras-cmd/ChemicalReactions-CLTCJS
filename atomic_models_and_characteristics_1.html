What is an atom?
~5 min
⚛ The atom

An atom is the smallest unit of matter that still has the chemical properties of an element.

Everything around you — air, water, your phone — is made of atoms. They are so small that a single grain of sand contains about 10 quintillion atoms.

💡 Key idea for this class

Scientists could not see atoms directly, so they used experiments to figure out what atoms look like. Each time they found new evidence, they updated the model. This is how science works.

Step 7 · Bridge
What comes next?
~3 min

Everything you learned today connects to these upcoming topics.

⚡

Electron Configuration

Z tells you how many electrons an atom has. Next we fill those electrons into Bohr's energy levels — shell by shell — following three rules.

📊

The Periodic Table

Elements are arranged by increasing Z. The row (period) = number of shells. The column (group) = number of valence electrons.

⚗️

Conservation of Mass

Atoms are never created or destroyed in a reaction — only rearranged. That is exactly why mass is always conserved.
